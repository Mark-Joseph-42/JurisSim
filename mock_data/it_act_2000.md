# Information Technology Act, 2000

## Section 2(1)(w): Intermediary
"Intermediary" with respect to any particular electronic records, means any person who on behalf of another person receives, stores or transmits that record or provides any service with respect to that record and includes telecom service providers, network service providers, internet service providers, web-hosting service providers, search engines, online payment sites, online-auction sites, online-market places and cyber cafes.

## Section 43A: Body Corporate
Where a body corporate, possessing, dealing or handling any sensitive personal data or information in a computer resource which it owns, controls or operates, is negligent in implementing and maintaining reasonable security practices and procedures and thereby causes wrongful loss or wrongful gain to any person, such body corporate shall be liable to pay damages by way of compensation to the person so affected.

## Section 69: Power to Intercept
The Central Government or a State Government may direct any agency to intercept, monitor or decrypt any information generated, transmitted, received or stored in any computer resource in the interest of sovereignty or integrity of India, defence of India, security of the State, friendly relations with foreign States, public order, or for investigation of any offence.

## Section 79: Intermediary Safe Harbour
An intermediary shall not be liable for any third party information, data, or communication link made available or hosted by him provided the function of the intermediary is limited to providing access, the intermediary does not initiate or modify the transmission, and the intermediary observes due diligence and removes unlawful content expeditiously upon receiving actual knowledge or government notification.
