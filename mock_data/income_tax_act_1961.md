# Income Tax Act, 1961

## Section 6: Residency
An individual is said to be resident in India in any previous year if he is in India for a period of 182 days or more during that year, or if he is in India for 60 days or more during that year and 365 days or more during the four years immediately preceding that year.

## Section 9: Income Deemed to Accrue in India
Income shall be deemed to accrue or arise in India if it accrues or arises through or from any business connection in India, or through or from any property in India, or through the transfer of a capital asset situate in India.

## Section 92: Transfer Pricing
Any income arising from an international transaction or a specified domestic transaction shall be computed having regard to the arm's length price. The arm's length price is the price which is applied or proposed to be applied in a transaction between persons other than associated enterprises in uncontrolled conditions.
